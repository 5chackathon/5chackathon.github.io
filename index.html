Overview

The 5C Hackathon is an entirely student-run event held at Pomona College. Historically, hundreds of Claremont College students gather to work on projects ranging from a Pac-Man clone controlled by the user’s face to an app designed for undocumented people to hear their rights and alert their loved ones upon being detained.

Our hackathon is a highly anticipated and attended tradition at the Claremont Colleges, drawing creative minds from all five schools in the consortium: Pomona, Harvey Mudd, Claremont McKenna, Scripps, and Pitzer.

What makes the 5C Hackathon different?

We pride ourselves on building a technical community at the Claremont Colleges that is both creative and diverse. With that in mind, we have a strong focus on making the 5C Hackathon beginner-friendly and inclusive. At our last event, 46% of participants were first-time hackers. We’ve co-hosted talks and workshops addressing issues of ethical coding and in the tech industry, and welcome exploration of these topics within student projects.

Teaching and mentorship are also of the utmost importance. We teach beginner and advanced workshops throughout the week preceding the hackathon, an event we lovingly call Hack Week. In the past, we’ve taught students how to make mobile apps using Android, web apps using Node.js, and digital art using P5. We also have mentors throughout the hackathon who provide technical and creative guidance.

Hack Week

Hack Week is a weeklong series of workshops teaching useful technical skills for both beginning and advanced coders and covering socially relevant issues within the world of tech and computer science.

When

Saturday, April 8 - Friday, April 14

Hack Week events are at various times in the afternoons and evenings starting the Saturday before the Hackathon and culminating in the 5C Hackathon on Friday.

What

Want to learn intro coding skills and data analysis? Or even the fundamentals of tech ethics? Hack Week workshops will help you learn how to build and host a website, explore data with Python, and use GitHub with your next project.

Code of Conduct

We value the participation of each individual and want to provide a positive experience for everyone. Accordingly, all attendees are expected to show respect and courtesy to other attendees. To make clear what is expected, all attendees, speakers, and volunteers are required to follow our Code of Conduct. Thank you for helping make this a welcoming, safe space for all!

Be Respectful

Be kind to others. Do not insult or put down other attendees. Behave professionally. Remember that harassment and racist, sexist, or exclusionary jokes or project content are not appropriate for this event and will not be tolerated.

Harassment includes but is not limited to: offensive verbal comments related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, race, or religion; sexual images in public spaces; deliberate intimidation; unwelcome photography or recording; inappropriate physical contact; and unwelcome sexual attention.

Be Open

We welcome attendees from all backgrounds and skill levels – from “never been to a hackathon before” to seasoned developers. This event is about increasing innovation, partnership, and engagement in the technical community on campus.

Be Awesome

Do your best. Learn something new. Don't take it too seriously.
